Milk-White Moon, Ink-Black Grins

Moonlight painted the cul-de-sac like spilled milk, and every shadow wore a grin. The paper-eyed clown on my mailbox blinked, ink tears sliding as the streetlamps flickered to a drumbeat I couldn't hear. When I waved back, my hands squeaked.
